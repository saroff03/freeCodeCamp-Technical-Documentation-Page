1. Restoration Shaman Overview

Restoration Shaman is an extremely flexible specialization that shines when healing groups of players that are clumped together. They have a variety of unique tools to deal with otherwise deadly influxes of damage, such as Spirit Link.

Because Chain Heal has no cooldown, Restoration Shamans are one of the few healers that can area-of-effect heal continuously when dangerous unexpected damage happens, a situation also perfectly suited to their Mastery effect: Mastery: Deep Healing.

The new Dragonflight talents allow for for high Mobility is not one of the specialization's strong points, periods of heavy movement can be handled by Spiritwalker's Grace. It is also notable that Restoration Shamans can deal significant damage with just instant casts through Flame Shock and Lava Surge-enabled Lava Bursts. These can easily be used in between heals with little loss to your healing output.

In order to properly play a healing role, you will need more than just class-specific information. To better understand healing in general, we recommend that you read our healing guide.

2. Strengths

  • Great spot and stacked group healing.
  • Can often cast while moving with Spiritwalker's Grace and jump around with Gust of Wind.
  • Excellent survivability with Astral Shift, Earth Elemental, and passives such as Brimming with Life.
  • Unique, powerful healing tools: Spirit Link Totem, Ancestral Protection Totem and Ancestral Vigor.
  • Great non-healing tools: Reincarnation, Wind Rush Totem, Wind Shear, Purge, Tremor Totem, Earthbind Totem, Earthgrab Totem, and Capacitor Totem.

2.1 Weaknesses

  • Loses some healing potential in situations that force a group to spread.
  • No teleport or immunity capabilities.
  • Non-cooldown healing is inefficient or tied to high cast times.
  • Mediocre baseline damage potential, with next to none of it coming from passive sources.
  • No significant tank cooldowns.

2.2 Notable changes for Dragonflight

The new talent system includes multiple powers from Shadowlands borrowed power systems, but also new additions and talents that call out to Battle for Azeroth and even older expansions, such as Earthliving Weapon.

The most notable returning talents are Legion's Ancestral Guidance and Gust of Wind, which provide extra cooldown healing and a way to counter deadly mechanics, especially when they involve knockbacks. Thundershock, Nature's Swiftness, Greater Purge and Spirit Walk are also interesting new additions, as is Acid Rain, which turns Healing Rain into a dual-purpose healing and damage spell!

Thundershock is also notable for providing a strong crowd control effect that we did not previously have access to, allowing you to be even more proactive with preventing enemy damage in dungeons and in certain raid scenarios.

Mobility is a problem of the past with Graceful Spirit being easily accessible alongside Wind Rush Totem, and Gust of Wind being an option for specific encounters or playstyles.

Finally, while dependent on tuning, Shaman has multiple strong healing talents for different healing scenarios: the raid-friendly Wellspring, the dungeon and PvP-friendly Ascendance, and Primordial Wave is an all-rounder tool which helps mitigate our traditional spread healing weakness.

3. Restoration Viability in Dragonflight

As mentioned before, tuning is the primary driver for a specialization to be desired in groups, and it is too soon to know whether Restoration Shaman will have favorable tuning or not in the early stages of Dragonflight.

That being said, the general Shaman talent tree has unmatched utility attached to it due to the immense amount of powerful, but circumstantial talents, such as Tremor Totem. These talents are meant to be swapped on a fight-to-fight basis if you are min-maxing, although it might be more convenient to just run them all and skip some throughput or survivability instead when heavily outgearing the content.

5. Basics of Restoration Shaman Gameplay

Restoration Shaman, like any healer, is all about using your abilities strategically. Rather than a fixed rotation, you will be using your flexible spell kit to adapt and overcome whatever the game throws at you.

Cooldown abilities, such as Riptide, Unleash Life and Healing Rain are very efficient, and thus important to use as many times as possible over the course of a fight, while the likes of Healing Wave and Chain Heal can be thrown in at any time they are needed, as long as you still have Mana left.
